<p><p>The cattle vaccine market encompasses various types, primarily categorized into live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and others. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, stimulating strong immune responses without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines consist of killed pathogens, providing safety but often requiring booster doses for effective immunity. Other vaccines may include subunit, mRNA, or vector-based types, offering alternatives that can target specific diseases with varied approaches to immune response and safety profiles, thus catering to diverse cattle health needs.</p></p>
