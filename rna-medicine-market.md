<p><p>RNA medicine encompasses various therapies utilizing different RNA-based technologies. siRNA (small interfering RNA) targets and silences specific genes to inhibit disease processes. ASO (antisense oligonucleotides) bind to RNA transcripts to modify gene expression and protein production. miRNA (microRNA) regulates gene expression post-transcriptionally, influencing various cellular functions. Nucleic acid aptamers are short, single-stranded RNA or DNA molecules that can bind to specific targets, such as proteins or small molecules, offering potential in diagnostics and therapeutics. Together, these innovations are transforming the landscape of medicine.</p></p>
