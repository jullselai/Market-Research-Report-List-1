<p><p>Gesture recognition in the retail market encompasses two primary types: touch-based and touchless systems. Touch-based gesture recognition involves interacting with screens or devices directly, allowing customers to browse products or access information through taps and swipes. In contrast, touchless gesture recognition captures movements without physical contact, enhancing the shopping experience by enabling users to interact with displays or kiosks from a distance. Both types aim to improve customer engagement and streamline transactions, creating a more intuitive shopping environment.</p></p>
