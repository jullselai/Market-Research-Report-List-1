Omnidirectional antennas provide 360-degree coverage, making them ideal for general communication across a wide area, such as in maritime or public safety applications. In contrast, directional antennas focus their radiation in a specific direction, enhancing signal strength and range for targeted communications, often used in broadcasting and point-to-point links.
